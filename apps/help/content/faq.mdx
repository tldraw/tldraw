## What is tldraw?

tldraw Inc. is the company that makes the tldraw SDK — a developer toolkit for building whiteboards and infinite canvas apps on the web. [tldraw.com](https://tldraw.com) is the team's own app built on that SDK. Visit [tldraw.dev](https://tldraw.dev) to learn about the SDK.

## Where are my files stored?

If you're **signed in**, your files are stored in the cloud and accessible from any device.

If you're **not signed in**, your drawing is stored locally on your computer using your browser's storage. Be careful: clearing your browser's storage will permanently delete your drawing.

## Can I export my files as backups?

Yes. Go to **Menu** → **File** → **Download file** to save any file as a `.tldr` file.

To re-import a `.tldr` file when signed in, drag it onto the sidebar or click your name in the bottom-left corner and choose **Import file…**. When not signed in, drag the file onto the canvas to open it (this replaces your current drawing).

You can also manage `.tldr` files with the [tldraw Visual Studio Code extension](https://marketplace.visualstudio.com/items?itemName=tldraw-org.tldraw-vscode).

## Can I self-host tldraw.com?

No. The tldraw.com app isn't designed to be self-hosted. If you want to run your own tldraw-powered app, use the [tldraw SDK](https://tldraw.dev) or start from a template like the [Vite template](https://github.com/tldraw/vite-template).

## Can I embed tldraw in my own website?

Not as a hosted product — tldraw doesn't offer an embeddable whiteboard service. However, you can use the [tldraw SDK](https://tldraw.dev) to integrate the canvas into your own React app. As a quick option, you can also embed a tldraw.com page in an iframe.
